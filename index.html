Formula 1 Explained

Introduction

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest level of single-seater car racing, where teams and drivers compete in fast, high-tech cars on tracks around the world. Each race is called a Grand Prix, and points are awarded based on finishing positions. The season’s goal is to win the Drivers’ Championship for the best driver and the Constructors’ Championship for the best team. It's a mix of speed, skill, strategy, and cutting-edge engineering.

Races

Each race is called a Grand Prix. Drivers earn points based on where they finish, and the driver and team with the most points at the end of the season become champions.
